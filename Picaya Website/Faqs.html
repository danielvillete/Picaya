Frequently Ask Questions

What is atsara?

Papaya is used in the preparation of atsara, an easy refrigerator pickle. It is commonly served as a condiment with grilled or fried foods.

What does atsara taste like?

Atsara is delicious, sour, and slightly crunchy. It has a sweeter taste than other pickles, and it can also be spicy.

What do you eat atsara with?

In Filipino cuisine, achara is commonly served with fried or grilled dishes. It can also be served as an appetizer or sandwich stuffing with nearly any dinner.

How long do you let atsara sit before eating?

For the best flavor and texture, brine the atsara for about three days. If you carefully slice the vegetables or briefly boil them in the brine, you may be able to eat it sooner. You can test their readiness by giving them a taste.

How do I know if achara has gone bad?

When the Atsara begins to smell bad, it must be thrown away. Molds, slimy or mushy pickles, murky or foamy brine, or bulging lids, for example, are all signs of degradation. If you are unsure, it is best to throw them away.

Does Atchara need to be stored in a certain way?

To keep the quality and freshness of Atchara, it's important to store it the right way. Put it in the refrigerator or keep it in a cool, dry place, and make sure the jar is tightly sealed.
